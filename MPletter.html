MP Letter Template

Inside a Pivotal Week for UK PPAN Science

How to write your local MP about the current PPAN crisis.

General Instructions

First of all, find your local MP, and start writing a letter to them using this platform. You just need to put in your postcode, and the website will track whether your MP answered to you or not.

Template

EMAIL TITLE: Save fundamental physics research in the UK

Dear MP NAME OF MP,

I am writing to you as a constituent and as an early-career researcher at [Name of University/Institute] to ask for your active support in ensuring that the recent open letter from early-career researchers to UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) is brought fully to the attention of Parliament.

The letter, available here: https://ecr-openletter-stfc.github.io/, has been signed by over 1,100 early-career researchers from across the UK and has already gained significant traction, including coverage in The Guardian (https://www.theguardian.com/science/2026/feb/06/uk-scientists-cuts-funding-projects-research-facilities).

The letter outlines the immediate and ongoing loss of young scientific talent from the UK. Researchers are already facing unemployment or being forced abroad because the postdoctoral job market has effectively collapsed, and existing UK fellowship schemes are not sufficient to compensate. This is not just speculative; it is a direct consequence of UKRI’s funding framework and, ultimately, decisions taken by the Government.

Without political pressure, there is a real risk that this letter does not receive the sustained attention it warrants, while the damage to careers and to the UK’s research capabilities continues. I am therefore asking you to bring this issue to the foreground: to raise it in Parliament, to press ministers and UKRI for a clear public response, and to ensure that the loss of a generation of scientists is treated as an urgent policy failure.

This matters locally as well as nationally and internationally. [constituency specific details: impact on local universities, schools, etc...]

I would be grateful if you could let me know what steps you are able to take to ensure that this letter receives the serious political attention it deserves.

D

Yours sincerely,

YOUR NAME
